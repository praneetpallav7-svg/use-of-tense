English Tense: Uses and Structures

Present Tense Past Tense Future Tense

Present Tenses

Simple Present

Structure: Subject + Base Verb (add -s/es for third person singular)

Uses:

  • Express habits or routines.
  • State general facts or truths.
  • Describe scheduled events.
  • likes and dislikes
  • saying and proverbs.
  • professions/jobs
  • time adverbials:always,never,seldom,rarely,scarcely,barely,everydar,etc
ExampleExplanation
She walks to school.Describes a regular habit.
The sun rises in the east.States a general fact.
He is a doctor.states a profession
honesty is the best policystates a proverb

Present Continuous

Structure: Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing

Uses:

  • Describe actions happening at the time of speaking.
  • Indicate temporary actions.
  • Express planned future events.
  • show gradual change
  • time adverbial:now,at the moment, mowadays,etc.
ExampleExplanation
I am studying now.Action happening at the moment.
They are traveling next week.Future plan.
the plants are growing gradually and slowlyshow gradual changes

Present Perfect

Structure: Subject + have/has + Past Participle

Uses:

  • Show past actions with present relevance.
  • Describe past experiences.
  • since nd for is used if main verb is know/have
  • time adverbial:already, yet, just, still, recently, etc
ExampleExplanation
I have just finished this.Recent completion with present impact.
She has visited Paris.Past experience.

Present Perfect Continuous

Structure: Subject + have/has + been + Verb-ing

Uses:

  • Emphasize duration of ongoing actions.
  • Show actions with present results.
  • time adverbials:since:point of time
    for:period of time
ExampleExplanation
I have been working all day.Emphasizes duration of action.
She has been learning English.Ongoing action with present relevance.

Past Tenses

Simple Past

Structure: Subject + Past Verb (regular: -ed, irregular: varies)

Uses:

  • Describe completed past actions.
  • Narrate past events or stories.
  • ago, yesterday, last+point of time, etc
ExampleExplanation
I visited London last year.Specific past action.
They played football.Completed past event.

Past Continuous

Structure: Subject + was/were + Verb-ing

Uses:

  • Show actions in progress in the past.
  • Describe background actions.
  • Indicate interrupted past actions.
  • all morning/day/evening/yesterday=at this time+past time
ExampleExplanation
She was reading at 8 PM.Action in progress at a past moment.
We were cooking when he arrived.Background action interrupted.

Past Perfect

Structure: Subject + had + Past Participle

Uses:

  • Show actions completed before another past event.
  • Clarify sequence of past events.
  • sequenve:
    when+past simple,past perfect
    simple past+after+past perfect
    past perfect+before+simple past
    as soon as+past perfect+simple past
ExampleExplanation
I had finished before she came.Action completed before another past event.
They had left when we arrived.Sequence of past events.

Past Perfect Continuous

Structure: Subject + had + been + Verb-ing

Uses:

  • Emphasize duration of past actions.
  • Show actions ongoing before another past event.
  • when/before+simple past
ExampleExplanation
She had been studying for hours.Emphasizes duration before a past event.
We had been waiting when it rained.Ongoing action before interruption.

Future Tenses

Simple Future

Structure: Subject + will + Base Verb

Uses:

  • Make predictions about the future.
  • Express spontaneous decisions.
  • State future facts.
  • time adverbials:tommorow/next day,week,etc/soon/tonight
ExampleExplanation
It will rain tomorrow.Prediction about the future.
I will help you.Spontaneous decision.

Future Continuous

Structure: Subject + will + be + Verb-ing

Uses:

  • Describe actions in progress in the future.
  • Indicate planned future activities.
  • time adverbials:
    at this time or moment+future time
ExampleExplanation
I will be working at 5 PM.Action in progress at a future time.
They will be traveling tomorrow.Ongoing future action.

Future Perfect

Structure: Subject + will + have + Past Participle

Uses:

  • Show actions completed by a future time.
  • Indicate future milestones.
  • time adverbial:
    by+point of time
    in+period of time
    before+point of time
ExampleExplanation
I will have finished by tomorrow.Completion by a future point.
She will have arrived by noon.Action completed before a future time.

Future Perfect Continuous

Structure: Subject + will + have + been + Verb-ing

Uses:

  • Emphasize duration of future actions.
  • Show ongoing actions up to a future point.
  • time adverbial:
    by+point of time
    in+period of time
    before+point of time
ExampleExplanation
I will have been working for 5 hours by noon.Emphasizes duration until a future point.
They will have been studying all week.Ongoing action up to a future time.
